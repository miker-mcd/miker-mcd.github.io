What Is Breadfruit?

Breadfruit is a grape-fruit sized fruit from the breadfruit tree and originates from New Guinea. The tree seeds spread by way of exploration of the South Pacific and were recognized for their high-yielding benefits. When cooked the texture of the fruit is similar to baked bread, thus giving the fruit its name. A sort of cousin of Jackfruit, Breadfruit tastes like a potato when ripe and is used as an ingredient in the same ways as potatoes.

The Next Superfood?

Breadfruit boasts many nutritional benefits along with their high-yielding quality, which is attracting the attention of the scientific community and organizations looking to resolve food security for people living in poor, tropical areas around the world.
